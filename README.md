This project analyzed the performance of two major Indian equity indices — NIFTY 50 and NIFTY Next 50 — over the period from 2015 to 2025. The objective was to evaluate whether the “innovation basket” (represented by NIFTY Next 50) delivered superior performance compared to the “mature market” (represented by NIFTY 50).

The analysis involved collecting historical price data for the ETFs representing these indices (NIFTYBEES and JUNIORBEES), calculating daily returns, and evaluating long-term performance through metrics such as CAGR, volatility, Sharpe ratio, and rolling volatility.

The results showed that while the NIFTY Next 50 generated slightly higher raw annual returns (14.19%) compared to the NIFTY 50 (13.56%), it also exhibited significantly higher volatility. As a result, the risk-adjusted performance of the NIFTY 50 was superior, with a higher Sharpe ratio.

This suggests that although the “innovation” segment of the market delivered marginally higher returns, the additional risk taken by investors was not sufficiently compensated. In contrast, the NIFTY 50 provided more stable and efficient long-term returns.

Overall, this analysis demonstrates the importance of evaluating investments not only on raw returns but also on risk-adjusted performance when comparing different segments of the market.
